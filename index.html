Email Best Practices

How to write subject lines, preheaders, and body copy that sound like CARET — plus the HubSpot setup steps that keep every send clean.

Before you write, answer three things

1

What is changing, available, or important?

2

Why does it matter to the firm?

3

What should the reader do next?

01

Subject lines

Short enough to work in a mobile inbox, specific enough to explain why the email matters.

Best range
30–50 characters
Upper limit
60 characters
Structure
5–9 words
Priority
Front-load the first 30–35 characters

Rules

  • Lead with the reader benefit, change, or action.
  • Use plain language.
  • Be specific, not clever for the sake of clever.
  • Skip all caps, excess punctuation, and vague urgency.
  • Don't repeat the preheader.
  • Use sentence case unless a product name needs caps.

Strong vs. weak

✓More usable data. Less time digging.
✓Plan your Outlook sync move
✓More visibility into firm performance
✗Exciting updates are here!
✗Unlock the full potential of your firm's data
✗Don't miss this important update from CARET Legal!!!
02

Preheaders

The preheader adds context to the subject line. It never repeats it.

Best range
40–80 characters
Upper limit
100 characters
Structure
One clear supporting sentence

Rules

  • Support the subject line — don't restate it.
  • Give the reader one more reason to open.
  • Front-load the useful information.
  • Skip vague filler like "Learn more inside."
  • Never default to "View this email in your browser."
  • Enter it manually in HubSpot.

03

Body copy

Most CARET comms emails should be short, focused, and easy to scan.

Simple comms
75–125 words
Product announcement
125–200 words
Operational email
100–175 words
Upper limit
250 words before it gets heavy

Structure

  1. Open with why it matters. Start with the reader's world, not the product.
  2. Explain the change. One short paragraph or a tight bullet list.
  3. Tie it to firm outcomes. Visibility, better decisions, less manual work, fewer gaps, stronger performance.
  4. End with one action. Every email gets a single, clear CTA.

Voice & tone

CARET sounds clear, confident, useful, and grounded — like a smart partner, not a software brochure.

Clear

Say what changed and why it matters.

Confident

Skip the hedging and the overexplaining.

Useful

Help the reader know what to do next.

Firm-focused

Lead with the operational or business impact for the firm.

Human

Sound like a smart partner, not a software brochure.

Grounded

Don't overpromise. CARET helps firms see, understand, manage, identify, review, and act.

05

HubSpot setup

CARET comms emails are sent through HubSpot, so platform setup is part of writing the email — not a final step tacked on after the copy is done.

Setup checklist

  • Internal email name follows the campaign naming convention.
  • Correct email type selected.
  • Associated with the right campaign.
  • Send list and suppression list are correct.
  • From name and from email match the message.
  • Subject line follows CARET standards.
  • Preheader entered manually, and doesn't repeat the subject.
  • CTA link is correct.
  • Tracking added where needed.
  • Personalization tokens have fallback values.
  • Previewed as a contact when personalization is used.
  • Test email sent, reviewed, and approved before scheduling.

Platform notes

Subject line. Enter the approved line directly into HubSpot — don't rely on a generated subject without editing it into CARET's voice.

Preheader. Always enter preview text by hand. Leave it blank and some clients will pull in image alt text, navigation, or body copy instead.

Personalization. Use it when it adds real clarity — firm name, contact owner, product usage, migration timing, event status, segment. Skip it when it doesn't.

Using personalization tokens

  • Confirm the data source is reliable.
  • Add fallback text.
  • Preview the email as a real contact.
  • Make sure the sentence still reads naturally if the fallback appears.

HubSpot QA pass

  • Subject line and preheader display correctly.
  • Mobile readability.
  • Header and hero spacing.
  • No broken or incorrect links.
  • CTA is clear.
  • Personalization tokens behave as expected.
  • Footer, unsubscribe, and compliance details are present.
  • No preview text accidentally pulled from alt text or body copy.

07

Final review

Confirm the email meets CARET copy standards and HubSpot QA standards before it sends.

  • Subject line is under 60 characters.
  • Preheader supports the subject and doesn't repeat it.
  • First sentence explains why the reader should care.
  • The body has one main message.
  • Bullets are limited to 3–5.
  • The CTA is clear and short.
  • The copy sounds calm, direct, and useful.
  • Claims are grounded and supportable.
  • HubSpot personalization has been tested.
  • Reviewed on both desktop and mobile.
  • All links work.
  • The correct send and suppression lists are selected.
