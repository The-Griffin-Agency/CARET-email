Email Best Practices

Clear, useful emails that help firms know what matters.

This guide outlines best practices for CARET comms emails, including subject lines, preheaders, body copy, voice and tone, and HubSpot setup.

The goal is to keep every email clear, firm-focused, and easy to act on.

Core principle

  1. What is changing, available, or important?
  2. Why does it matter to the firm?
  3. What should the reader do next?

Subject Line Best Practices

Subject lines should be short enough to work in mobile inboxes, but specific enough to tell the reader why the email matters.

Recommended length

  • Best range30 to 50 characters
  • Upper limit60 characters
  • Ideal structure5 to 9 words
  • PriorityPut the most important words in the first 30 to 35 characters

CARET subject line rules

  • Lead with the reader benefit, change, or action.
  • Use plain language.
  • Be specific, not clever for the sake of clever.
  • Avoid all caps, excessive punctuation, clickbait, or vague urgency.
  • Avoid repeating the preheader.
  • Use sentence case unless a product name requires capitalization.

Strong examples

Billing, payments, and accounting in CARET

Weaker examples

02

Preheader Best Practices

The preheader should add context to the subject line. It should not repeat it.

Recommended length

  • Best range40 to 80 characters
  • Upper limit100 characters
  • Ideal structureOne clear supporting sentence

CARET preheader rules

  • Support the subject line.
  • Give the reader one more reason to open.
  • Front-load the useful information.
  • Avoid vague language like “Learn more inside.”
  • Never default to “View this email in your browser.”
  • Enter it manually in HubSpot.

03

Body Copy Best Practices

Most CARET comms emails should be short, focused, and easy to scan.

Recommended length

  • Simple comms email75 to 125 words
  • Product announcement125 to 200 words
  • Operational email100 to 175 words
  • Upper range250 words before it starts feeling heavy

Recommended structure

  1. Open with why it matters. Start with the reader’s world, not the product.
  2. Explain the change clearly. Use one short paragraph or a tight bullet list.
  3. Tie it to firm outcomes. Connect the message to visibility, better decisions, less manual work, fewer gaps, or stronger firm performance.
  4. End with one clear action. Each email should have one primary CTA.

Opening comparison

Good

From intake to billing to reporting, data sits behind every part of the practice.

Less good

We’re excited to announce a new analytics feature in CARET Command.

HubSpot Setup Best Practices

Because CARET comms emails are sent through HubSpot, every email should be reviewed in both copy and platform context before it goes live. HubSpot setup should not be treated as a final production step only. It is part of the comms process.

Setup checklist

  • The internal email name is clear and follows the campaign naming convention.
  • The correct email type is selected.
  • The email is associated with the right campaign.
  • The send list is correct.
  • The suppression list is correct.
  • The from name and from email match the message.
  • The subject line follows CARET standards.
  • The preheader is entered manually.
  • The preheader does not repeat the subject line.
  • The CTA link is correct.
  • Tracking is added where needed.
  • Any personalization tokens have fallback values.
  • The email has been previewed as a contact when personalization is used.
  • A test email has been sent and reviewed.
  • The final email has stakeholder approval before scheduling.

Platform notes

Subject line

Enter the approved subject line directly into the HubSpot subject line field. Do not rely on generated subject lines without editing them into the CARET voice.

Preheader

Always enter preview text manually in HubSpot. If the preheader is missing, some email clients may pull in unintended text from the email, including image alt text, navigation, or body copy.

Personalization

Personalization should feel useful, not forced. Use it when it adds clarity or relevance, such as firm name, contact owner, product usage, migration timing, event registration status, or customer segment.

When using personalization tokens

  • Confirm the data source is reliable.
  • Add fallback text.
  • Preview the email as a real contact.
  • Make sure the sentence still reads naturally if the fallback appears.

HubSpot QA review

  • Subject line display
  • Preheader display
  • Mobile readability
  • Header and hero spacing
  • Broken or incorrect links
  • CTA clarity
  • Personalization token behavior
  • Footer, unsubscribe, and compliance details
  • Any accidental preview text pulled from image alt text or body copy

07

Final Review Checklist

Before sending, confirm the email meets CARET copy standards and HubSpot QA standards.

  • The subject line is under 60 characters.
  • The preheader supports the subject and does not repeat it.
  • The first sentence explains why the reader should care.
  • The body has one main message.
  • Bullets are limited to 3 to 5 max.
  • The CTA is clear and short.
  • The copy sounds calm, direct, and useful.
  • Claims are grounded and supportable.
  • HubSpot personalization has been tested.
  • The email has been reviewed on desktop and mobile.
  • All links work.
  • The correct lists and suppression lists are selected.
